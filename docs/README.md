# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome! This documentation repository contains the core program management processes and practices used at OctoAcme for effective, reliable project delivery.

## Overview of Project Management Processes

The project management processes utilized by OctoAcme revolve around structured workflows, well-defined roles, collaborative communication, and a focus on quality. These processes are optimized for cross-functional collaboration, iterative delivery, and clear accountability, ensuring projects are executed efficiently and with measurable outcomes. The project lifecycle is divided into distinct stages—initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective—to guide teams from conceptualizing ideas to delivering customer value. This lifecycle emphasizes alignment with stakeholders, manageable increments of work, and continuous improvement.

Key workflows include the use of a project board to track progress through stages such as Backlog, Ready, In Progress, QA, and Done. Pull request workflows focus on maintaining small, reviewable changes with clear acceptance criteria, automated testing, and approvals before merging. Quality assurance plays a significant role, incorporating unit tests for new logic, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows, and manual QA for feature acceptance when required. Metrics like velocity, burndown, and product success indicators are monitored regularly, ensuring that progress aligns with business goals.

Roles within the OctoAcme framework are clearly defined to ensure accountability and coordination. The Project Manager (PM) oversees delivery, risk management, and communication, while the Product Manager (PdM) focuses on goals, priorities, and outcomes. Developers, QA/Testers, and stakeholders contribute by implementing, validating, and providing input or approvals, respectively. Collaboration between these personas is supported by structured communication such as daily standups, weekly syncs, and stakeholder updates, ensuring consistent alignment and problem resolution.

To maintain high standards, OctoAcme prioritizes clarity in processes, documentation, and continuous feedback. Retrospectives held at the end of milestones encourage teams to identify actionable improvements. Risk management follows a lifecycle of identification, assessment, mitigation, and monitoring, with defined escalation paths in place for critical issues. Through regular retrospectives, defined roles, and detailed workflows focusing on productivity and quality, OctoAcme delivers consistent results while cultivating a culture of learning and adaptability.
