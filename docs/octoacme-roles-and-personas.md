# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. Developers are expected to engage early in requirements discussions and flag technical constraints proactively to avoid rework.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Collaborate with QA Specialists on testability and defect resolution
- Partner with UX Designers to implement interaction designs accurately

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed
- Defect triage sessions with QA Specialists

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes. They serve as the primary voice of the customer and are responsible for ensuring that the team is solving the right problems in the right order.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics
- Partner with UX Designers to incorporate user experience considerations
- Work with Business Analysts to document and refine requirements
- Review and sign off on acceptance criteria alongside QA Specialists

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs
- UX review sessions and design critiques

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently. Project Managers work closely with Scrum Masters in Agile environments to ensure that process execution aligns with delivery goals and stakeholder expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication
- Collaborate with Scrum Masters to maintain Agile delivery cadence
- Engage Business Analysts to ensure requirements are captured and traceable

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation
- Sprint review summaries for leadership

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates Agile practices, removes impediments, and helps ensure the team follows agreed process norms. The Scrum Master is a servant leader who focuses on team health, continuous improvement, and adherence to Agile principles rather than on directing the work itself.

### Responsibilities
- Coach the team on Agile methodologies and best practices
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers; escalate when necessary
- Shield the team from unplanned interruptions and scope creep
- Track sprint metrics (velocity, burndown) and surface trends
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety
- Collaborate with Project Managers to align process with delivery commitments

### Goals
- Maintain a healthy, predictable delivery cadence
- Continuously improve team processes and collaboration
- Reduce friction and waste in day-to-day workflows

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Impediment logs and retrospective action items
- Velocity and burndown reports shared with Project Managers
- Coaching conversations with individual team members

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
The UX Designer ensures user needs are incorporated into features by designing wireframes, prototypes, and interaction flows. They bring usability best practices and user research into all stages of the project, acting as the advocate for the end user throughout the product lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize user requirements through research and interviews
- Produce wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define information architecture and interaction patterns
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature requirements
- Work with Developers on implementation details and design feasibility
- Partner with QA Specialists to validate that delivered features match design intent
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design standards

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce usability defects discovered late in the cycle
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- Prototype walkthroughs and usability test findings
- Design handoff documentation (specs, assets, interaction notes)
- Accessibility audit reports

## Quality Assurance (QA) Specialist

### Role Summary
QA Specialists define, automate, and execute tests to ensure deliverables meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. They flag regressions early, verify fixes, and advocate for testability throughout the lifecycle. QA Specialists play a critical role in maintaining the integrity of releases and protecting the end-user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain test plans, test cases, and test scripts
- Execute exploratory, regression, and performance tests
- Report, track, and verify defects through to resolution
- Collaborate with Developers to define acceptance criteria and testable requirements
- Automate regression suites to support continuous delivery
- Validate UX and accessibility against design specifications
- Participate in sprint ceremonies to provide quality perspective

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Reduce the cost of quality by catching issues early
- Build confidence in each release through thorough validation

### Typical Communication
- Defect reports and triage sessions with Developers
- Test summary reports shared with Project Managers and Product Managers
- Acceptance criteria refinement sessions
- Release readiness sign-off communications

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical teams. They document requirements, analyze workflows, and support solution design to ensure that the delivered product aligns with organizational goals and user expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document requirements, user stories, and business rules
- Model workflows and map existing and future-state business processes
- Analyze impact and feasibility of proposed changes
- Support User Acceptance Testing (UAT) by defining scenarios and coordinating stakeholders
- Maintain a requirements traceability matrix to track coverage
- Liaise between stakeholders, Product Managers, and Developers to clarify goals

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are complete, unambiguous, and traceable
- Reduce misalignment between business expectations and delivered solutions
- Support smooth handoffs between discovery and delivery phases

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- User story backlog grooming sessions with Product Managers
- Process flow diagrams and business rules documentation
- UAT coordination with end users and QA Specialists
